Point Gap & New Caps


The Relationship Between the Point Gap per Match
and Uncapped Players

This plot sought to answer the question if the presence of uncapped players (debutant players) would have an effect on a match's point margin. The assumption is that a higher number of new caps would result in a lower positive point margin. Each dot on this plot represents a match, and it is easy to see that a bulk of matches are played without any rookies. The expectation is to see a downward slope of the dots as the number of new caps increase, but instead, the markers taper towards the center as the number of uncapped players increase. To understand why this may be, further analysis would need to be done on the nature of each match, such as considering which team the All Blacks were playing and how many new caps the opposition team fielded themselves.
