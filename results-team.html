This chart shows the overall record the All Blacks have against each international side. Of the 21 teams played, they have only lost to 6 teams and are undefeated against 15. Of those 6 teams (Australia, England, France, Ireland, the British & Irish Lions, and South Africa), South Africa boasts the most amount of wins (10) over the All Blacks, with Australia coming in second (8). This is not a surprise as New Zealand plays these two teams the most often, as all three are apart of the same championship tournament.

However, when looking at the percentages of match outcomes, one can see that the British & Irish Lions have also given the All Blacks a run for their money. This is because 1) the All Blacks only play the Lions 3 matches every 12 years, meaning that there is only data on 6 matches in this data set, and 2) the Lions are a team comprised of all the star players from England, Scotland, Wales, and Ireland. A combination of all the top players of four top-tiered teams not only makes the Lions more competitive, but the small data set also results in distorted percentages.
